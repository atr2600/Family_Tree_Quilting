    Military Experience

    My military career started when I was 17 years old. It lasted 8 years before I decided to get out and go back to school. During my time in the military, I was stationed at two bases and deployed two times.

    Army Reserves 2008-2010 (New Orleans, LA)>

    In 2008, I was a junior in high school and I decided to enlist in the Army Reserves. The Army, at the time, had a split option program. This program allowed high school students to go to basic training during the summer time and finish training after one's senior year. After high school, I was assigned to 377th Theatre support command at the Belle Chase Naval Air Station. I requested a transfer to active duty during the summer of 2010.

    Army 2010-2013 (Fort Lewis, WA)

    My request to transfer to active duty was approved and my first assignment was Fort Lewis, WA. I was placed in the 593rd Sustainment Brigade, 57th Transportation Battalion, Headquarters and Headquarters detachment. My job title was unit level armor and supply clerk. I was responsible for maintaining all weapons for my company. This included quarterly maintenance and services.

    One year after being assigned to the unit the battalion was scheduled for deactivation. Most of the companies attached to this battalion would laterally move to a sister battalion. Unfortunately, the headquarters company would have to take care of all the battalions equipment. I was tasked with deactivating the company. I was given an Army Commendation Medal for my work deactivating the unit as a Private First Class.

    After the unit was deactivated I was transferred to the 593rd Brigade Headquarters company in order to deploy to Afghanistan with them. We were apart of the Centcom Material Recovery Element (CMRE). Our job was to retrograde the equipment in Afghanistan. Afghanistan’s geography makes it unique compared to other combat zones. Afghanistan is landlocked and does not have any railway system within the country. Everything that is in Afghanistan came by air and air is a expensive means of transportation. It was our job to come up with a plan to get all the vehicles, rolling stock, and other equipment, non-rolling stock out of the country.

    Army 2013-2016 (Fort Hood, TX)

    Once I got back stateside I was transferred to Fort Hood, Texas. I was assigned to several Engineer units including the 8th Engineer Combat Battalion and 62nd Engineer Battalion, 104th Engineer Company.

    During 2014, there was an outbreak of Ebola in East Africa and our unit was assigned to build Ebola treatment facilities. We deployed to Monrovia, Liberia and our camp was in a small police training camp outside the main city. Our unit was not responsible for the medical side of the military mission but we did get a chance to talk and interact with the locals.

    After that deployment, I decided to get out the military and go back to college. I loved my time in the military but It was hard on the family. I was accepted at the University of Minnesota Duluth and started my new career in Computer Science.
